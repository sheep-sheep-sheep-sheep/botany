The Overseers

Froth entered the dimly lit office, Taking a seat across from the man. "I’ve been told the names, but not the histories," she began. "So....who are the Overseers, really?" The man across from her didn't look up from his drink. "A collection of accidents and opportunists, mostly." He gestured to her file. "Pick a number."

13

"Ah, Thirteen," the man smiled, not reaching his eyes. "Formerly Fourteen, until the vacancy opened up. He’s the 'Man of God' finally granted a divine vote. He’s one of the two hold-overs. The power he learned and the knowledge he possesses are.....well significant. If only he’d stop leaving his Bible in my office, we’d get along better."

11

"'Leven?" the man waved a hand dismissively. "The Ghost. The Shadow. The Unknown. To be honest, even I’m not entirely sure how he secured the seat. You needn't worry about him, though; the man seems far too bored to be dangerous." "What? You said you knew everyone!" Froth interjected. The man scoffed. "In this building, 'Nobody' is often your best bet for an identity."

10

"Ten, or Jackson, as he likes to be called." the man flipped a silver coin. "He was military might personified. He used to command Alpha-1 'The Red Right Hand' itself." Froth leaned in, eyes wide. "He was MTF?" "The only smart one in the unit," the man chuckled. "He realized that dying for the Council was a sucker’s game. During the insurgency attack, he used the chaos to secure his own seat. Now? He tells others to go die for him."

2

"Two is... the .aic," the man said, his voice softening. "That’s a cold way to put it. She was a person once, but she’s fading. The last 'true' member of the old Council, fading away in a silicon tomb." "That’s... horrible." The man took a slow sip of his drink. "The rest of the Council already has her replacement lined up. A woman from the Deletions Department, I believe. Efficiency over empathy." Froth felt her throat dry.

7

"And Seven?" Froth asked. The man blinked, appearing surprised. "Seven is a shark. A capitalist. He was a Tier-Zero buyer for Marshall, Carter & Dark before he realized he could own every anomaly in existence if he just helped the Foundation out of debt." "MC&D?!" Froth nearly snapped her pen. "We’re letting the merchants run the shop?" "The Foundation needed a way to rebuild the destroyed Sites quickly. He provided the capital; they provided the seat. A simple transaction."

12

"Twelve? A lying bastard," the man said, setting his glass down with a heavy thud. "But she’s the reason the Foundation didn't descended into total mass hysteria. She was the Head of Memetics" the man gaze hardened "She knows exactly how to ruin a memory. She’s convinced the entire organization that 'everything is fine' while the world burned."

4

"I heard Four was different," Froth whispered, unsure. "That they were.....uhm kind?" "The Junior Researcher. The youngest to ever hold the title." the man let out a dry scoff. "She’s humane to a fault. She’d probably try to pet a reality-eater if it looked sad enough. But don't mistake that for weakness. She’s the 'Heart.' And you can’t remove the heart without the body dying, now can you?"

8

"Eight is a traitor at heart," the man continued, fidgeting. "He was the liaison between us and the Global Occult Coalition. " "H-he wh-what?" Froths voice dies in her throat. "He defected the moment he saw the seats were up for grabs. He still has the GOC convinced he’s an undercover spy. Personally? I think he’ll jump ship to the Chaos Insurgency the moment they land another heavy blow."

6

"Uh, Six! What about him?" "**Them.**" the man stares. "They came from the Library. One of the 'Serpent's Hand' crowd." Froth flinched, covering her mouth. "A Library member? A Jailbreak artist?" "They ran into the Jailer's cage the second they realized they could be the ones holding the keys."

3

"...Snake got your tongue? Let’s try a different note. Three." Froth looked away, unable to meet his gaze. "A good, honest man," the man said, his grin uncomfortably wide. "He shot his boss, took the elevator keycard, and told security exactly why he did it." The man leaned back. "I believe he said; 'I refuse to die for a man who would let me.'"

5

"Five is the only one with a lick of common sense," the man sighed. "Former Ethics Committee. When the old world went to hell, the last Five’s secretary came to him, begging him to lead." "And that’s common sense?" Froth tilted her head. "No," the man laughed sharply. "Common sense was the fact that he refused. He only took the job once he realized how much of a disaster the rest of these council was."

1

"Ah, One." the man stood up. "The one with the Spear, the one who fought in the blood, the one who met the Administrator." "..." "...The Usurper." the man gazed out a closed window. "...You may have your opinions on how he took power, but it was necessary. He kept the lights on while everyone else was busy looting the wreckage."

9

"Wait!" Froth interrupted. "What about Nine." "...Nine?" the man paused, thinking. "Right..... That's an odd number choice." The man sighed. "The prize to the most efficient site director each month." the man smirked. "I don't believe a single one won the seat twice, a month of godhood changes a man." the man turned to leave.

"I see." Froth lowered her head. "Don't look so grim, Froth. You’ll make an great Archivist. History needs someone to write it down, even if the truth is ugly." "Thank you for telling me this... Mr...?" The man paused at the door frame, glancing back. "Please just call me Nine."
